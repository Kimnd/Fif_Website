I guess that’s kind of obvious, him being the Receiver, but still. All the other Sixteens (Jonah always points out the day that we were born for some reason, so I know my age better than most) are pretty easy to get to know after a short amount of time, even me! But Jonah’s deeper than that.

Like this one time when we were Sevens, we were all playing a game, and I was on Jonah’s team, but he wasn’t doing anything, just standing there, looking at something. I was upset at his lack of interest in the game—I remember ‘cuz I talked about it during Feelings—but when I asked him what he was looking at, he said “I apologize for not focusing on the game, Asher. I was just looking at the trees. They’re different somehow today, don’t you think?”

I frowned, looking at the trees. “They don’t look distant… what do you mean?”

Jonah shook his head, giggling, “Different, Ash. Not distant.”

“I apologize for not—”

“—you’re fine! Anyway, I mean they look like…” Jonah lapsed into one of his precision of language hazes, and being a Seven I groaned impatiently. But I waited, Jonah always came out of these eventually. I spent the time looking more closely at the trees. They really didn’t seem different, but then again when had I ever even noticed them? It’s not like they change their locations each day like people. I heard somebody call our names.

“Hey, Ash, Jonah, are you still playing?”

“We apologize for causing you anything inconvincing, but me and Jonah are tired.”

He laughed a little—guess I had botched that sentence too—but looked disappointed. “I accept your apology. But it’s not really fun without you…” And we both knew that I wouldn’t play if Jonah didn’t. So we both looked at him.

He was still peering at the trees really hard, muttering under his breath once in a while. He looked so grown-up, I snickered before turning back and shrugging. “I’ll play next time! Promise.” As the boy turned away, Jonah got the Ahha look that indicated the end of his episode.

“It’s like somebody changed the light on the trees so that the lighted parts are more brightly lit, and the parts that aren’t lit are even more dark.” Jonah beamed in his Jonah way, not like he was really proud that he’d said something so fancy and smart, but in the way that he was just happy being able to describe what he wanted. I wanted to be able to share everything with Jonah, but this was holding it back!

Apparently this showed on my face, because we spent the rest of playtime leaning against the fence together while Jonah described what he was seeing and what he had seen before. Eventually I did understand it, when Jonah compared it to the difference in what you can see during the day and during the night, or when the light in your room is on or off before bed. During the day, there aren’t very many shadows because there’s light everywhere. Nighttime has lots of shadows and darkness.

That’s what’s amazing about Jonah, he sees everything in a way that you can spend so much time trying to understand. Even before Jonah was the Receiver, it was like he saw everything with a little more detail than anyone else I knew, even my Parents and Instructors. They see objects or people that are related to them in some way, what was relevant to them or to me. Jonah just sees.

But anyway, the dreams started way earlier for me than they did for my Mother and Father, so I guess I was earlier than most people. I didn’t really have any of the words to describe how the dreams felt, so I just told my Mother and Father about how the dreams felt warm and that Jonah had been there. It took them a few times to actually realize what the dreams meant, my wording was so convoluted.
